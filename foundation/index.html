Virtual Research Foundation

Expanding access to serious mentored research.

Virtual Research Foundation is an access initiative in formation, created to expand scholarship support for students with demonstrated financial need who are ready for serious mentored research.

Overview

Scholarship access for serious student research.

The Foundation's purpose is to help qualified students participate in rigorous online research mentorship when cost would otherwise be a barrier.

Purpose

Need-based access

Scholarship support is intended for students whose families could not otherwise afford advanced mentored research opportunities.

Scope

Research mentorship

The Foundation is focused on access to mentor-guided research experiences in mathematics, statistics, data science, computer science, and related fields.

Access Mission

Opening doors for capable students.

Many students are ready for serious academic work but lack access to specialized mentorship, university networks, or family resources.

Need

Financial need

Scholarship support would prioritize students with demonstrated financial need and clear interest in sustained academic work.

Readiness

Academic seriousness

Support would be directed toward students prepared to engage thoughtfully with mentor-led research, collaborative work, and meaningful intellectual challenge.

Stewardship

Responsible use of funds

Scholarship processes should be objective, documented, and centered on student access and educational benefit.

Relationship to VRI

Independent roles, shared educational purpose.

Virtual Research Institute operates mentored research programs. Virtual Research Foundation is being formed as a separate scholarship-focused entity to support access for students with financial need.

VRI

Program operator

  • Designs and operates mentored research programs
  • Coordinates mentors, cohorts, schedules, and student experience
  • Charges tuition for educational services
Foundation

Scholarship support

  • Supports students with demonstrated financial need
  • Uses scholarship criteria and documented review processes
  • Focuses on student access rather than operating VRI programs
Current status: Virtual Research Foundation is in formation. It is not currently accepting public donations or representing tax-exempt status. Future scholarship activity will depend on appropriate legal, governance, and financial-aid structures.
