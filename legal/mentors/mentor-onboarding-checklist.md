# Mentor Onboarding Checklist

**Document status:** Internal draft for operations and onboarding  
**Prepared for:** Virtual Research Institute LLC  
**Intended use:** Working checklist for mentor approval, onboarding, proposal review, engagement confirmation, and activation

---

## 1. Purpose

This checklist is intended to help VRI confirm that a mentor has completed the key approval, documentation, payment, compliance, proposal-readiness, launch-readiness, and activation steps required before beginning work under VRI.

This checklist distinguishes between:

- approval as a VRI mentor;
- submission or public listing of a mentor proposal;
- confirmation that a specific proposal or project will launch;
- authorization to begin live or participant-facing work.

Approval as a mentor does not by itself guarantee assignment, active work, proposal launch, participant-facing work, or compensation.

## 2. Status of Document

This draft is a working internal operational document and may be revised as VRI’s onboarding process evolves.

## 3. Core Onboarding Items

The following items should generally be completed before a mentor is activated for VRI work:

- signed Mentor Independent Contractor Agreement;
- signed confidentiality / non-circumvention terms or equivalent provisions;
- completed tax documentation (such as Form W-9, if applicable);
- approved payment setup for direct deposit;
- verified legal name and contact information;
- verified location and time zone;
- reviewed VRI communication, professionalism, and safeguarding expectations;
- reviewed documentation and recordkeeping expectations;
- reviewed proposal-pipeline, enrollment-interest, and launch-status confidentiality expectations;
- confirmed understanding that a one-year mentor agreement does not guarantee assignment, proposal launch, active work, or compensation.

## 4. Screening and Verification

Where applicable or required by VRI, the following items should be completed or documented before mentor activation:

- identity verification;
- CV / resume review;
- publication, credential, or affiliation review where relevant;
- reference review where relevant;
- background check completion where required or appropriate;
- confirmation that no material misrepresentation or disqualifying concern has been identified.

Approval remains discretionary even if individual screening items are completed.

## 5. Proposal and Readiness Review

Before a mentor is considered for a specific VRI engagement, the following items should generally be completed where relevant:

- submitted project proposal or approved project concept;
- identified pathway relevance (Circles, Groups, or Private Mentorship continuation, if applicable);
- stated prerequisites, scope, expected outputs, and student preparation assumptions;
- identified proposed session length, approximate workload, and expected student deliverables;
- confirmed availability for the relevant term or engagement window;
- confirmed ability to operate through approved VRI platforms and channels;
- confirmed understanding of session expectations, continuity expectations, and participant-boundary requirements;
- confirmed understanding that VRI may edit, approve, reject, publish, unpublish, postpone, revise, merge, or retire a mentor proposal at its discretion.

Submission, approval, editing, or public listing of a proposal does not by itself guarantee assignment, launch, student enrollment, participant-facing work, or compensation.

## 6. Public Listing and Proposal-Pipeline Controls

If a mentor proposal, mentor profile, topic description, project page, or related offering is publicly listed or prepared for public listing, VRI should confirm that:

- the mentor understands that public listing does not mean the project is guaranteed to run;
- the mentor understands that VRI-generated student interest, family inquiries, waitlists, proposal-page traffic, enrollment status, and launch decisions are protected VRI business information unless VRI makes them public;
- the mentor will not represent that a listed proposal is guaranteed to run unless VRI expressly authorizes that representation;
- the mentor will not use VRI proposal pages, VRI-generated inquiries, student/family leads, or waitlist information to promote or fill non-VRI services;
- the mentor will not redirect students, families, applicants, leads, or proposal-page inquiries into private tutoring, consulting, mentoring, courses, or other non-VRI arrangements without VRI’s written authorization;
- the mentor understands that VRI may limit the number of active proposal pages, listed offerings, pending projects, or participant-facing assignments associated with the mentor.

## 7. Launch Readiness Review

Before VRI confirms that a specific proposal or project will run, VRI should generally review the following, where applicable:

- enrollment level;
- student fit and preparation;
- pathway fit;
- mentor availability;
- scheduling and time-zone feasibility;
- operational readiness;
- program balance and breadth of offerings;
- scholarship, funding, or payment considerations where relevant;
- quality-control concerns;
- safeguarding or participant-protection concerns;
- whether the proposal should run as listed, be revised, be merged with another offering, be moved to a later session, or be retired.

If a proposal does not have sufficient enrollment or does not otherwise meet VRI’s launch conditions by the relevant deadline, VRI may move the proposal to a later session, revise it, merge it with another offering, keep it listed for future interest, or retire it.

Before moving a proposal into a later session or revised engagement window, VRI should reconfirm the mentor’s availability and determine whether any proposal edits, updated dates, revised scope, or additional approvals are needed.

## 8. Engagement Confirmation

Before a mentor begins live or participant-facing work for a specific VRI engagement, VRI should generally issue or document a written engagement confirmation, addendum, assignment notice, or comparable approval.

The engagement confirmation should identify, where applicable:

- mentor name;
- pathway or engagement type;
- project title or approved topic;
- session or engagement window;
- expected start date and end date;
- approved schedule or scheduling process;
- compensation amount or calculation method;
- payment timing or installment structure;
- required records and documentation;
- approved platforms and communication channels;
- authorized scope of work;
- any project-specific conditions, limits, or modifications.

A written engagement confirmation helps distinguish general mentor approval from an active, approved, participant-facing assignment.

## 9. Final Activation

Before a mentor begins live or participant-facing work for VRI, VRI should confirm that:

- onboarding documentation is complete;
- screening is complete to the extent required;
- payment setup is complete;
- the mentor is approved for the relevant engagement;
- the relevant proposal or project has been approved to launch;
- any required engagement confirmation, addendum, assignment notice, or comparable written approval has been completed;
- the mentor has been told the current scope of authorized work;
- the mentor has reviewed communication, safeguarding, confidentiality, non-circumvention, documentation, and no-private-side-deal expectations;
- the mentor understands that approval for one term, cohort, proposal, listing, or engagement does not automatically authorize other work without VRI approval.

Final activation is discretionary and may be delayed, limited, or withheld if VRI determines that additional review, clarification, enrollment, scheduling confirmation, documentation, or operational approval is needed.
